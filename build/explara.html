Discovering Local Experiences On The Go Made Simple With Explara

RobinStrategy and UX
ChaitanyaInterface Design
RakeshFront-end Development
8Weeks
6meetings
53commits

Explara is a very special client for us and we have been working with them as a design partner for over 3 years now. We have been there as a design partner right from the time when they were an early stage startup with a five people team that used to share office space with us to being a 50+ people team with global presence. Along the journey came many pivots to the business model from being a event ticketing platform to being a personalised experiences platform, and now again shifting focus towards being a suite of apps for all event organisers. We have worked together with Explara team as their extended design team. Here is a glimpse of how Explara has evolved over the last few years:

Showcasing Movies on the homepage

These were designed for the launch of movie ticketing on Explara and show a snap of latest movies in the city on home. Unfortunately, due to some business changes this feature couldn’t launch but has been one of our favorite design works for them.

Common Search for experiences, communities and topics

New navigation was designed to ease discovery. By choosing the right colors, position and layout we managed to give search its due importance though occupying very little space.

Not in a mood for Trek ? How about rafting or camping

While looking for interesting stuff to do over a weekend, users are often confused and want to explore more. They might be in a mood for a trek but in case they don’t find interesting, we wanted to make them think of other possibilities like Rafting, Camping etc. We wanted to make sure they find something they like on the site easily. Hence, we used the top band with a visual representation of categories.

Allowing organisers to accept bookings on their site with Widgets

Nobody likes leaving a site to another to complete a booking. It reduces trust and authenticity of the third party site and hence we came up with an idea of an embeddable widget where users can complete entire booking on the organisers website with a one page checkout, without even creating an account. It can also be customised with the organisers website with smart use of an accent color.

Increasing signups by the yellow band for new users landing on topic pages

One of the key promises of the platform was to offer personalized experience and recommendations. Yellow bands recommending users to signup or login was a neat but powerful way of increasing signups without obstructing the flow.

Capturing interests on signups for a personalized experience

Sign Up process encouraged Facebook signups and helped users indicate their interests and personalized app experience accordingly.
